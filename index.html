About Me


I am a motivated fresh graduate with exposure to fast-growing startup scene seeking an opportunity to grow professionally and develop challenging solutions. I completed my MS in Computer Science from San Francisco State University. I worked on "Analysis and Prediction of Walking Performance using Large Scale Data" as my applied research project. Implementing state-of-the-art Machine Learning techniques I achieved an AUC score of 85%.

I served as a Software Development Intern for six months at Backpack Technologies, Inc., a Y-combinator backed e-commerce company. I was responsible for end-to-end development and maintenance of a number of features for the web application and internal tools. This role required a strong capability in handling ambiguous and challenging problems with time and resource constraints.

Working on a number of projects throughout my professional and academic career made me adept at a wide variety of technologies ranging from Java, Python, MySQL to React, Node.js, Django, Laravel, Git, AWS, Tableau and many more. My goal is to apply my knowledge to improve people's lives and make the world a better place.

Ceres

Ceres

Ceres is a web application that helps users minimize food waste by informing them of the amount and expiration dates of the food they possess. It provides functionalities such as the ability to digitally share a fridge among multiple users, creating shared shopping lists, reading the food item names from a receipt using OCR, searching for recipes based on existing items on the fridge etc. As the team-lead, my key contributions include - designing the product features and database schema collaborating with developers and stakeholders; setting up and maintaining the web server and MySQL database on AWS; improving the quality of the final product and codebase by reviewing and providing constructive feedback on pull requests. As a full-stack developer, my notable contribution to the project was development of the front and backend of the shared fridge and shopping lists feature. It included sending and responding to invitations for sharing a fridge, maintaining items on the shopping list and more. Technologies used in this project are Git, Python, Django, MySQL, AWS, NGINX, uWSGI, Bootstrap, HTML, CSS.
